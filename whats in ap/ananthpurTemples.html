Ahobilam Temple

Ahobilam Temple, also known as Ahobalam or Ahobalam, is a significant Hindu temple complex dedicated to Lord Narasimha, an incarnation of Lord Vishnu. It is located in the Nallamala Hills of the Nallamala Forest, in the Nallamala range of the Eastern Ghats, in the Ananthapur district of Andhra Pradesh, India. Historical Background

Legend: According to Hindu mythology, Ahobilam is believed to be the place where Lord Narasimha manifested to protect his devotee Prahlada and to destroy the demon Hiranyakashipu. The temple is considered one of the 108 Divya Desams, which are sacred abodes of Lord Vishnu. Establishment: The temple complex consists of several shrines dedicated to different forms of Narasimha, and it has been a pilgrimage site for centuries. The current structure was developed during the Vijayanagara Empire.

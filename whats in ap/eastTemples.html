Annavaram

Historical Significance

The temple was consecrated on August 6, 1891, following a divine vision experienced by a Brahmin named Eeranki Prakasa Rao. The idol was discovered on Ratnagiri Hill, illuminated by sunlight beneath a Krishna Kutaja tree. The temple has undergone several renovations, with significant reconstruction occurring in the 1930s.

Draksharama

Draksharama Temple, also known as Bhimeswara Swamy Temple, is a revered Hindu shrine located in Draksharamam, East Godavari district, Andhra Pradesh. Here are some key details about the temple:

Historical Background

The temple is dedicated to Lord Bheemeswara Swamy, a form of Lord Shiva, and is one of the five Pancharama Kshetras, as well as one of the 18 Shakti Peethas. It is believed to have been constructed between the 9th and 10th centuries CE by the Eastern Chalukyan king, Bhima. The temple is historically significant as it is associated with the legend of Daksha Yagna, where Sati's left cheek is said to have fallen, leading to the temple's establishment.

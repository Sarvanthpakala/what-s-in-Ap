Amareswara Temple

The Amareswara Temple, often associated with the Amaralingeswara Temple, is located in Amaravati, Guntur District, Andhra Pradesh, rather than East Godavari. This ancient temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva and is situated on the southern bank of the Krishna River. It is one of the five Pancharama Kshetras, where Lord Shiva is worshipped in the form of a Shivalinga.

Historical Significance:

The temple is believed to have been established by Lord Indra, and it holds a significant place in Hindu mythology.The Sivalinga here is notable for its height, requiring archakas to use a pedestal platform for daily rituals.A unique feature of the Sivalinga is a red stain at the top, which is said to be the result of a nail hammered in to stop its growth, leading to blood oozing from it.

Jain Temple

Shri Hrinkar Jain Tirth:

Located on the Vijayawada Guntur Highway in Namburu, this temple is dedicated to Lord Mahavir and is known for its serene environment and beautiful architecture.

Architecture: The temple features intricate carvings and a peaceful ambiance, making it a popular spot for meditation and prayer. Festivals: The temple hosts various festivals throughout the year, attracting devotees from nearby areas.

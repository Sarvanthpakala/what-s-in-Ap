Vontimitta Temple

The Vontimitta Temple, also known as the Kodandarama Temple, is a significant Hindu temple located in Vontimitta town, Kadapa district, Andhra Pradesh. Dedicated to Lord Rama, it is renowned for its stunning architecture and historical importance. The temple was constructed around the 16th century, initially by the Chola rulers and later by the Vijayanagara rulers. Here are some key points about the temple:

The temple is believed to have been established by Lord Indra, and it holds a significant place in Hindu mythology.The Sivalinga here is notable for its height, requiring archakas to use a pedestal platform for daily rituals.A unique feature of the Sivalinga is a red stain at the top, which is said to be the result of a nail hammered in to stop its growth, leading to blood oozing from it.

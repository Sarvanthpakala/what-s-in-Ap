Bhairavakona Temple

Bhairavakona is a significant religious and tourist site located in the Nallamala Hills of Prakasam district, Andhra Pradesh, India. It is particularly renowned for its temple dedicated to Lord Bhairava, a fierce manifestation of Lord Shiva. The site is characterized by its scenic beauty, historical significance, and spiritual ambiance, making it a popular pilgrimage destination.

Overview

Deity: The temple is primarily dedicated to Lord Shiva, who is worshipped here in the form of Uma Maheswara, symbolizing the union of Lord Shiva and Goddess Parvati.

Location: The temple is situated in the scenic Nallamala Hills, approximately 30 kilometers from Kurnool city. The serene environment and lush greenery surrounding the temple enhance its spiritual ambiance.

Narayana Swamy Temple

The Narayana Swamy Temple is a prominent Hindu temple located in the town of Kovilampadu in the Prakasam district of Andhra Pradesh, India. This temple is dedicated to Lord Narayana, a form of Lord Vishnu, who is revered as the preserver and protector in Hindu mythology. The temple attracts numerous devotees and is known for its spiritual significance and architectural beauty.

Ramatheertham

Ramtheerdham is a significant pilgrimage site located near Chimakurthi in the Prakasam district of Andhra Pradesh, India. It is dedicated to Lord Ramalingeswara Swamy, a form of Lord Shiva, and is known for its spiritual significance, serene environment, and historical importance.

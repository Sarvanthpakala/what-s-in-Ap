Bhairavakona Temple

Bhairavakona is a significant religious and tourist site located in the Nallamala Hills of Srikakulam district, Andhra Pradesh, India. It is particularly renowned for its temple dedicated to Lord Bhairava, a fierce manifestation of Lord Shiva. The site is characterized by its scenic beauty, historical significance, and spiritual ambiance, making it a popular pilgrimage destination.

Overview

Deity: The temple is primarily dedicated to Lord Shiva, who is worshipped here in the form of Uma Maheswara, symbolizing the union of Lord Shiva and Goddess Parvati.

Location: The temple is situated in the scenic Nallamala Hills, approximately 30 kilometers from Kurnool city. The serene environment and lush greenery surrounding the temple enhance its spiritual ambiance.

Sri Venkateswara Swamy Temple

Deity: Lord Venkateswara (a form of Lord Vishnu) Significance: This temple is dedicated to Lord Venkateswara and is popular among devotees. It is known for its beautiful architecture and vibrant festivals. Location: Located in the heart of Srikakulam town.

Srikurmam temple

Deity: Lord Vishnu (in the form of Kurma, the tortoise) Significance: This ancient temple is one of the few temples dedicated to Lord Kurma, the second avatar of Lord Vishnu. It is believed to be over 1,000 years old and is known for its unique architecture and sculptures. Location: Approximately 16 km from Srikakulam town.
